Light caramel frappe

What is frappe?

A frappe is a cold, blended beverage made with ice, coffee, milk, and sweeteners. It's typically made by blending the ingredients until smooth, creating a creamy, frothy drink that can be customized with flavors like chocolate, vanilla, or caramel.

Frappes are popular as a refreshing treat, especially during warm weather, and are often served at cafes or coffee shops. They can be topped with whipped cream, syrups, or sprinkles, making them a versatile and indulgent drink option.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups ice cubes
  • 1 cup 2% milk
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee, chilled
  • 2 tablespoons sugar-free caramel topping
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 (1 gram) packets granular sucralose sweetener, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fat-free frozen whipped topping, thawed
  • 1 tablespoon sugar-free caramel topping

Steps

  1. Combine ice, milk, coffee, 2 tablespoons caramel topping, vanilla extract, and sucralose sweetener in a blender. Pulse until ice is crushed to a smooth and thick consistency.
  2. Pour into a mug or tall glass and decorate with whipped topping.
  3. Drizzle 1 tablespoon caramel syrup on top.
